- InGaN thin films with 38-68% indium content studied

## Preliminary Assessment

Based on the research question background and available data:

### Tellurium Constraints
- Global production: ~500 tonnes/year (byproduct of copper refining)
- Single 1 kmÂ² CdTe unit requires 5,000-7,000 kg Te
- **Critical finding**: One large unit = 1-1.4% of world annual supply
- Supply is inelastic to price - cannot easily scale

### Indium Constraints
- Global production: ~900 tonnes/year (byproduct of zinc refining)
- 70% production concentrated in China
- Required for ITO regardless of cell chemistry (CdTe, CIGS, perovskite)

### Implications for Dyson Project
1. **CdTe at scale is infeasible** without asteroid Te sources or Te-free alternatives
2. **ITO alternatives critical** - must qualify AZO/FTO/alternatives for space
3. **Dual-track development** wise - pursue both terrestrial and ISRU paths
4. **Perovskite priority** may be driven by material constraints, not just mass

The preliminary assessment suggests CdTe scaling is fundamentally constrained by Te availability, making alternative cell chemistries or ISRU sourcing mandatory for Dyson-scale deployment.
