# Asteroid Mining with Small Spacecraft and Economic Feasibility

## Abstract

This paper examines using small spacecraft (under 500 kg) for asteroid mining operations, proposing water extraction through microwave heating at 2 Wh/g efficiency.

## Key Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Spacecraft mass | <500 kg |

| Required lifetime | 5+ years operational |

## Water Extraction Method

- **Technique**: Microwave heating of regolith
- **Energy requirement**: 2 Wh/g water extracted
- **Processing**: In-situ on asteroid surface
- **Storage**: Cryogenic or compressed

## Swarm Architecture Benefits

1. Risk distribution across multiple units
2. Parallel processing capability
3. Graceful degradation on unit failure
4. Incremental deployment possible
